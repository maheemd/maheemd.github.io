About me

Bridging technical systems with economic evidence and real-world impact

Energy Economics Green Hydrogen Clean Energy Transition Technology Diffusion CO₂ Emissions Analysis Input–Output Analysis Electricity Forecasting Python & Data Analysis Robotics & IoT Technology Entrepreneurship

I am an interdisciplinary energy researcher with academic training in applied economics and electrical and electronic engineering. My research focuses on green hydrogen, clean energy transition, technology diffusion, electricity-demand forecasting, CO₂-emissions reduction and the macroeconomic impacts of emerging energy technologies, particularly in developing and data-scarce economies.

Before entering graduate research, I spent more than eight years as a founder and project leader, developing and delivering projects in robotics, IoT, electronics and industrial systems. This experience shaped my practical approach to research: analytical models should not only be technically rigorous, but also address real policy, market and implementation challenges.

Today, I combine economic analysis, engineering knowledge and entrepreneurial experience to study how new technologies can move from technical possibility to large-scale adoption. Through this website, I also document and share my research, technical projects, learning resources and practical knowledge so that they remain useful to students, researchers and future innovators.
